A Few words about us

Eshibanze Muslim Secondary School was a brainchild idea of four community members namely Mr. Abdallah Malala, Hassan Khamala, Athman Mbago and James Were Mapao, who came up with the idea of starting a Muslim Secondary School to quench the thirst for secondary education, especially to our mother primary school. The school admitted four students on January 24, 2011, and thus began the long journey to where we are today. Mr. Rashid Nambwaya was appointed acting Principal until the Teachers Service Commission appointed Mr Japhary Omari Nyangweso as the school's first T.S.C teacher and principal in 2012. In 2012, the school was registered as a co-educational public institution with both Muslim and non-Muslim students. Enrollment at the school has enormous potential. It is now registered with the Ministry of Education as a mixed Muslim Day School sponsored by the Muslim Community. The school currently has an enrollment capacity of 380 students. Mrs. Emelda Otieno was appointed by the TSC in 2019 to take over the leadership of the school as Principal from Mr. Japhary Nyangweso. The infrastructure of the school has developed as a result of her wise leadership skills, and the school has continued to post good results, resulting in an increase in demand and enrolment of the student population. The school is now officially a three-streamed day school in Matungu Sub County.
